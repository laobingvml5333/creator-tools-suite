How YouTube Earnings Work

YouTube earnings are primarily based on CPM (Cost Per Mille), which is the amount advertisers pay for every 1,000 views of their ads on your videos. However, not every view is monetized. Factors like viewer location, niche, and video length affect your actual RPM (Revenue Per Mille).

What affects your CPM?

  • Niche: Finance and Tech channels often have higher CPMs ($10+) compared to Vlogs or Gaming ($1-$3).
  • Geography: Views from countries like USA, UK, and Australia pay significantly more.
  • Seasonality: Ad rates typically increase in Q4 (October - December).
