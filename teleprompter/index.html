What is an Online Teleprompter?

An online teleprompter (also known as an autocue) is a professional tool designed for content creators, speakers, video bloggers, and live streamers. It helps you read pre-written scripts smoothly while recording videos, delivering speeches, or streaming live, without the need to memorize large amounts of content. For YouTube creators, corporate trainers, online educators, and public speakers, a teleprompter is an essential tool for improving content quality and delivery fluency. Our Free Online Teleprompter provides a clean, intuitive interface with powerful features, allowing you to focus on your content rather than worrying about forgetting your lines or stumbling over words.

  • Smart Scroll Control: Adjustable scrolling speed from 1-10 levels, allowing you to customize the pace according to your reading habits and speech rhythm.   • Flexible Font Sizing: Supports font size adjustment from 20px to 100px, adapting to different viewing distances and vision needs. Whether you're using a laptop up close or a large screen from a distance, you can find the most comfortable reading size.

How to Use This Free Teleprompter Tool

  1. Prepare Your Script: Paste or type your speech, video script, or live stream content into the text box in the editing area. You can input text of any length, and the tool will automatically save it to your local browser.
  2. Click "Start Prompter": When ready, click the blue "Start Prompter" button in the top right corner. The page will immediately switch to full-screen prompter mode, displaying your script in large, centered text.
  3. Adjust Display Settings: In the bottom control bar, you can adjust font size (drag the "Font Size" slider) and scrolling speed (drag the "Speed" slider) to find the reading configuration that works best for you. The WPM display helps you understand your reading pace.
  4. Start Recording and Play: Click the green play button (or press spacebar), and your script will begin auto-scrolling upward. You can press spacebar at any time to pause, adjust your pace, and continue playing.
  5. Use Advanced Features: If you need mirror display, click the purple "Mirror" button; if you want to start over, click the red "Reset" button; when finished recording, click "Exit Prompter" to return to edit mode.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is my script data safe?

Absolutely safe! All text you input is stored only in your own browser's local storage (LocalStorage) and is never uploaded to any server or cloud. We cannot access your data, nor do we collect any personal information. This is a completely client-side tool, guaranteeing your privacy and content security.

Can this tool work offline?

Yes! After your first visit, the browser automatically caches all necessary files (HTML, CSS, JavaScript). Even if you disconnect from the internet, you can still open this page and use all features normally. Your saved text and settings will also remain in your local browser.

Does it work on mobile phones or tablets?

Fully supported! This tool uses responsive design and works perfectly on screens of any size. Whether you're using an iPhone, iPad, Android phone, or tablet, you'll get a smooth user experience. It's especially suitable for use on iPads with external cameras for video recording.

How do I use this with a physical teleprompter device?

If you have professional teleprompter glass (one-way mirror) equipment, simply click the "Mirror" button to enable horizontal flip. Text will display mirrored on screen, and after reflection through the glass, you'll see normally oriented text in front of the camera. This is a common configuration in professional video production.

How accurate is the estimated duration?

The estimated duration is calculated based on your current speed setting and total word count. Speed level 1 corresponds to approximately 100 WPM (slower pace), while speed level 10 corresponds to approximately 300 WPM (faster pace). The default speed 5 is set to ~150 WPM, which is a comfortable conversational pace. Adjust the speed slider to see how it affects your estimated reading time.
